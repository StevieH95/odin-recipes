Indian Lamb Curry

Description

This authentic Indian lamb curry is made with lamb shoulder. It’s simmered with fragrant spices, caramelized onions, and Greek yogurt until fork-tender. Served over Indian bread or basmati rice, this is a meal on its own.

Ingredients

  • Lamb
  • Onions
  • Garlic and ginger
  • Yogurt
  • Spices
  • Spice mix

Steps

  1. In a Dutch oven or heavy bottom skillet, add the oil, ghee, and whole spices
  2. Then, add onions and saute until caramelized, almost brown – about 10 minutes.
  3. Next, add the ginger and garlic – saute for another minute. Add the lamb pieces, and saute for 2 to 3 minutes until the lamb is no longer pink.
  4. Then, add the ground spices and chopped tomatoes. Saute for 2 to 3 minutes until fragrant
  5. Next, add the yogurt. Combine it then add 1/2 cup of water. Season with salt, black pepper, and lemon juice. Bring to a boil on medium-high heat.
  6. Continue to simmer on the stovetop on medium-low heat, stirring frequently until meat is cooked through and becomes fork-tender – about 1 to 1 ½ hour adding water if necessary.
  7. Pro tip – Always ensure you have enough water so the meat has enough moisture to cook. If necessary add ¼ to ½ cup more.
  8. When cooked to fork-tender, taste and adjust seasoning. Adjust gravy by adding a bit more water depending on how you going to serve it
  9. Garnish with fresh cilantro or parsley.
